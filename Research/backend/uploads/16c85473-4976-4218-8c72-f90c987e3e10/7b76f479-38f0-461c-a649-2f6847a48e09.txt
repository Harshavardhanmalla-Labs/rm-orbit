This paper investigates sparse attention mechanisms in transformer models. We propose a novel top-k sparse attention routing algorithm that reduces quadratic complexity to near-linear. Our experiments on BERT-base and GPT-2 show 40% reduction in compute while maintaining 98.2% of baseline accuracy. Key findings: (1) Sparse attention with top-k=64 reduces FLOPs by 42.3% versus full attention, (2) Local window attention (window=128) retains 97.3% of full attention quality on GLUE benchmarks, (3) Our hybrid local-global approach achieves state-of-the-art on 6 of 8 GLUE tasks with F1=89.4. Methods: We fine-tuned BERT-base on PyTorch 2.0 with gradient checkpointing enabled. Training ran for 3 epochs with lr=2e-5 and batch size 32. Dataset: GLUE benchmark suite comprising 8 tasks and 67,432 training examples. Evaluation: We measured FLOPs per forward pass using torch.profiler and tracked validation loss across 3 runs with different random seeds.
