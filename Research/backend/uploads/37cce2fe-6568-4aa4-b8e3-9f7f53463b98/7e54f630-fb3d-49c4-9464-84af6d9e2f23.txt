This paper investigates attention mechanisms in transformer models. We propose a novel sparse attention approach that reduces quadratic complexity. Our experiments on BERT and GPT-2 show 40% reduction in compute while maintaining 98% of baseline accuracy. Key findings: (1) Sparse attention with top-k selection reduces FLOPs by 42%, (2) Local window attention retains 97.3% of full attention quality on GLUE benchmarks, (3) Combined approach achieves state-of-the-art on 6 of 8 GLUE tasks. Methods: We fine-tuned BERT-base on PyTorch 2.0 with gradient checkpointing. Dataset: GLUE benchmark suite (8 tasks, 67k training examples). The key contributions are: a new attention routing algorithm, sparse masking heuristic, and a hybrid local-global attention scheme.
